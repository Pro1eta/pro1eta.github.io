6G：More Sustainable Future

Every Coin Has Two Sides

Many years later as she looked up at the Huawei's advertisements exhibitted on the big LED screens, My mother was about to remember that distant exiting evening when she bought her first smartphone in the 2010s. There's no doubt that the rapid development of communication technologies(CT) has reshaped the society we're living in. Only in China, more that 805 million people[1]has reaped big benefits of 5G including faster downloading speed and lager communication range.

However, every coin has two sides. The applications of 5G have also brought threat to the environment, which may affect our living in a negative way. One thing we cannot ignore is that frequent upgrades of communication facilities such as PCs, smartphones and base stations, catering for the fast development of CT, is causing more and more electronic waste. According to the report from the WHO[2], merely in 2019, the recognized first year of 5G, 53.6 million tonnes of e-waste were produced globally, but only 17.4% was documented as formally collected and recycled. What we should also pay attention to is the high energy consumption. "Thus, under the constuction of 5G, the energy consumption is several times or even dozens of times more than 4G's." said an acdemical artical with the data below.[3]

6G: A Possible Sustainable Future

What is always a consensus is that in order to develop our society healthily, we have to be very cautious about technological development and environmental protection. Luckily, when we are on the way of stepping into a new are of 6G, more approaches are offered to better balance the two, from which we may take a glance of a sustainable future of 6G.

1.New Materials

The technology of semi-conductors has laid a foundation of 5G, and so will do 6G. According to the white paper of 6G by Huawei[4],"Based on the existing silicon platform — one that is already mature — advanced process features are added to enable new capabilities...The advancements of semi-conductor technologies have made it possible for us to achieve a THz integrated circuit..." To speak very very simply, it means lower cost, being efficient and lower heat emitting. That is, generating ways causing polution will be upgraded revolutionarily, recycling can be more doable and less energy will be used for air-conditioners.

2. Factory of The Future[5]

6G could also bring many other benefits to the factory of the future. For example, a ubiquitous RF[6] sensing system would enable proactive maintenance of the entire production environment and processes, and, as the factory of the future requires no human onsite, lights-out manufacturing would significantly lower the OPEX(i.e cost) and carbon footprint.

Ending: The Future Has Come

The future has come. We envision that 6G will enable the transformation from connected people and things to connected intelligence, And the sustainability is undoubtedly one of the critical pillars.
